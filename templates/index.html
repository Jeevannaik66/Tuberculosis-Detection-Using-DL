Recognize TB Symptoms

Early detection saves lives. Learn to identify the key symptoms of tuberculosis for timely intervention

Persistent Cough

Lasting over three weeks, often with mucus or blood. This results from lung tissue damage and inflammation caused by TB bacteria.

Chest Pain

Sharp or dull pain that worsens with coughing or deep breathing. Caused by inflammation and infection in lung tissues.

Fatigue & Weakness

Overwhelming tiredness that doesn't improve with rest. The immune system's constant battle against TB drains energy reserves.

TB Prevention Guide

Essential practices to protect yourself and others from tuberculosis infection

Do's - Protective Measures

Seek Medical Attention

Consult healthcare providers immediately for persistent symptoms. Early diagnosis enables effective treatment and prevents complications.

Complete TB Testing

Undergo comprehensive testing if exposed to TB. Early detection through skin tests, blood tests, and chest X-rays saves lives.

Wear Protective Masks

Use N95 masks in healthcare settings and crowded areas. Proper masking significantly reduces airborne transmission risk.

Practice Respiratory Hygiene

Cover mouth when coughing/sneezing. Use tissues or elbow to prevent droplet spread. Dispose of tissues safely.

Don'ts - Risk Avoidance

Don't Ignore Symptoms

Never dismiss persistent cough, fever, or weight loss. Delayed treatment increases complications and transmission risks.

Avoid Close Contact

Maintain distance from active TB patients. TB spreads through airborne droplets in enclosed, poorly ventilated spaces.

Avoid Crowded Spaces

Steer clear of poorly ventilated, crowded areas. TB bacteria can linger in the air for hours in enclosed environments.

What is tuberculosis and how does it spread?
Tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious bacterial infection caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It primarily affects the lungs but can spread to other organs. TB spreads through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or speaks.
How accurate is AI-based TB detection?
Our deep learning model achieves over 95% accuracy in detecting tuberculosis patterns from chest X-rays. It's trained on thousands of validated medical images and continuously improves through machine learning.
What are the early warning signs of TB?
Early signs include persistent cough (3+ weeks), mild fever, night sweats, fatigue, and unexplained weight loss. Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes.
How can I protect my family from TB?
Ensure good ventilation at home, practice respiratory hygiene, maintain healthy immunity, and seek immediate testing if exposed. BCG vaccination provides partial protection in high-risk areas.
Is tuberculosis completely curable?
Yes, with proper antibiotic treatment over 6-9 months, TB is completely curable. Strict adherence to medication is crucial to prevent drug-resistant strains.

Protection Strategies

Comprehensive approaches to safeguard yourself and your community from tuberculosis

Vaccination & Immunization

BCG vaccination provides crucial protection, especially for children in high-prevalence regions. Consult healthcare providers for vaccination recommendations.

Hygiene & Sanitation

Regular handwashing, respiratory etiquette, and proper ventilation significantly reduce transmission risks in households and communities.

Personal Protection

Use appropriate masks in high-risk settings, maintain social distancing, and avoid crowded, poorly ventilated spaces whenever possible.

Healthy Lifestyle

Balanced nutrition, regular exercise, adequate sleep, and stress management strengthen immune systems against TB infection.
